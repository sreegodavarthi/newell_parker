Parts

Is it possible to get a spare part?

While we do offer a nib exchange service and do sell most fountain pen nibs and rollerball sections, unfortunately we do not sell other spare parts.
We do not sell caps or barrels for the following reasons:

  • The replacement of the required cap or barrel might not be the only repair needed
  • For pens that are not black, we may experience color matching issues between the cap and the barrel
  • All repair operations do include an overhaul which might be a pen cleaning, a mechanism check, and replacement of cartridge and convertor for fountain pens.

Will you exchange my nib for free if I want another nib size?

We do offer a nib exchange service. Fine and medium nibs are available for all of our fountain pen models. If you are interested in another nib size please determine the size required and contact us at 800-237-8736.
If the nib we receive is in perfect condition and if the new required nib belongs to the same model we will carry out the nib exchange free of charge. Otherwise you will receive a quote.
We do recommend that you return the complete pen in order to protect the nib during transit.
Please be sure to include all of your contact information with the pen so that we may reach you easily. For products offered or purchased more than 10 years ago, we ask that you send a digital picture of your pen so we can identify the model.

How do I have my writing instrument repaired?

For current models:

  • Please be sure to include a description of the repair you are requesting with your writing instrument.
  • We recommend that you send us your pen by Insured Mail, FedEx or UPS to enable us to easily track your package.
  • Please keep the tracking # in your records so that you can check that we have received your package
  • Be sure you include your contact information, warranty document and/or your printed sales receipt with your writing instrument.
  • Also recommend that you do not send your pen in its original gift box, but wrap it safely in bubble wrap and enclose it in a padded envelope or box.

For older models purchased more than 10 years ago:

  1. Please send us an email at SAS.US@newellco.com with a digital picture of your pen so we can determine the model.
  2. We will confirm with you next steps based on the age of the writing instrument.
  3. If the repair is covered by the warranty and we cannot repair or replace the writing instrument with exactly the same product, we may contact you before carrying out the replacement of the pen for free. We may offer you:
    • Another color, same pen
    • Same color, nearest current model
    • Nearest color, nearest model
  4. The repair is not covered by the warranty and we cannot repair or replace your writing instrument, we will return it to you unrepaired and offer you a Special Discount of 20% on a pen of your choice that is currently available. The offer will be valid for 2 months.

How do I refill my fountain pen?

Please see the refill instructions below:
1. Cartridge insert:

  • Insert the cartridge into the gripping section of the barrel. Apply slight pressure until you feel and hear the cartridge seat into place.
  • The gripping section will pierce the cartridge, allowing the ink to flow into the feed assembly.
  • Screw the bottom section of the barrel on over the cartridge into the gripping section of the barrel.
2. Converter insert:
  • Insert the open end of the convertor into the gripping section of the barrel.
  • Twist or slide the black end of the convertor (plunger) down to the end inserted into the gripping section.
  • Dip the nib of the fountain pen into the bottled ink so that the nib is submerged slightly below the gripping section.
  • Remove the nib from the bottled ink and twist or slide the plunger slightly to force out three drops of ink back into the bottle.
  • Twist or slide the plunger back up to the top position to draw a little air into the converter.
  • Screw the bottom section of the barrel on over the convertor into the gripping section of the barrel.
  • Use a soft cloth to wipe excess ink off the nib and body.

How do I clean my fountain pen?

  • We do recommend cleaning your fountain pen between each ink refill.
  • If a pen clogs, place the nib and gripping section of the barrel in a cup of cool water.
  • Let the nib and gripping section soak overnight.
  • Our inks are water-soluble and will dissolve with water
  • Remove the gripping section from water and rinse it with a slow stream of cool water.
  • Gently blow into the nib assembly to remove any excess water.
  • You may now place a new ink cartridge or an ink convertor into the gripping section of the barrel.

How shall I store my fountain pen to get its best performance?

  • The nib should be stored point up when not in use.
  • The ink will drain down into the convertor or cartridge.
  • This avoids drying or clogging of the nib
